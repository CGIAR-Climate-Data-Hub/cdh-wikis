# Grey-literature inventory — CMIP6 Africa wiki v2

This worksheet catalogues the **non-journal authoritative sources** the v2 wiki draws on. Grey literature here means: regional climate centre publications, NMHS technical bulletins, UN-system reports, development bank technical guidance, and CGIAR programme deliverables. These voices are what make the wiki feel *African-grounded* rather than just rebranded global literature.

**Convention** per entry: Organisation · Type of publication · Most recent / most relevant publications · How to access · "What to cite this for"

**Status**: initial seeding 2026-05-28. Active outreach to several organisations pending — entries marked *(outreach pending)* are placeholders for contributions the wiki will solicit directly.

---

## Continental & pan-African organisations

### WMO Africa Regional Office

- **Type**: UN-system, technical authority for African meteorological / climate services
- **Most relevant publications**:
  - **State of the Climate in Africa 2024** (WMO-No. 1349, published May 2025). The most recent continental authority. African temperature anomaly +0.86 °C vs 1991-2020 baseline; North Africa fastest-warming (+1.28 °C); extreme rainfall events 2024 documented (Sahel floods, Kenya MAM floods). [WMO SOC Africa 2024](https://wmo.int/publication-series/state-of-climate-africa-2024)
  - **State of the Climate in Africa 2023** (WMO-No. 1360). [PDF on UNECA-ACPC](https://www.uneca.org/sites/default/files/ACPC/publication/State-of-the-Climate-in-Africa-2023_en.pdf)
  - **Statement on State of Global Climate 2024** (WMO-No. 1368). Global context.
- **Access**: [wmo.int publications](https://wmo.int/publication-series)
- **What to cite this for**: Observed climate context for Africa (any "what has happened so far" framing). The State of the Climate Africa reports are the headline observational reference.

### WMO Regional Climate Centres for Africa (RCC network)

- **Type**: WMO-designated centres providing operational climate services
- **Members for Africa**: ACMAD, ICPAC, SADC-CSC (each covers a sub-region)
- **What to cite this for**: That the climate-services architecture exists; that NMSAs use CMIP-based products operationally; that the Atlas's audience overlaps directly with these centres' constituency.
- **Access**: [WMO RCC info](https://community.wmo.int/en/activity-areas/climate/climate-services/regional-climate-centres)

### ACMAD — African Centre of Meteorological Applications for Development

- **Location**: Niamey, Niger. Continental coverage.
- **Type**: Continental seasonal outlooks + early warning + climate-services capacity-building
- **Most relevant publications**: continental seasonal-forecast bulletins; African Climate Monitor *(outreach pending — confirm latest CMIP6-specific publications)*
- **Access**: [ACMAD website](https://www.acmad.org/)
- **What to cite this for**: ACMAD-level seasonal-forecast methodology; continental rainfall climatology / monsoon overview; the framing the AU + Member States use.

### UNECA-ACPC — African Climate Policy Centre

- **Location**: Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. UN Economic Commission for Africa.
- **Type**: Climate-policy framing + technical support for African government climate planning
- **Most relevant publications**: *Climate Risk and Adaptation in Africa* series; technical briefs on NDC implementation
- **Access**: [UNECA ACPC](https://www.uneca.org/african-climate-policy-centre)
- **What to cite this for**: How African policy-makers frame climate science; what NDCs use; the policy-translation layer between climate science and adaptation planning.

### AfDB — Africa Adaptation Initiative

- **Type**: African Development Bank's funding-side adaptation programme
- **Most relevant publications**: AAI Compendium; African Climate Change Programme reports
- **Access**: [Africa Adaptation Initiative](https://africa-adaptation-initiative.net/) · [AfDB Climate Action](https://www.afdb.org/en/topics-and-sectors/sectors/climate-change)
- **What to cite this for**: Funder-side framing of climate adaptation; how proposals are evaluated; the financial flows the Atlas's audience writes proposals into.

## Sub-regional centres

### ICPAC — IGAD Climate Prediction and Applications Centre

- **Location**: Nairobi, Kenya. Greater Horn of Africa coverage (IGAD member states + neighbours)
- **Type**: WMO-designated Regional Climate Centre. Operational seasonal forecasting; climate-change information services.
- **Most relevant publications**:
  - **CMIP6 Climate Change Information Visualization Portal** — ICPAC's operational CMIP6 portal for East Africa. The Atlas's closest operational analogue in the region. [icpac.net climate-change page](https://www.icpac.net/rcc/climate-change/)
  - **Greater Horn of Africa Climate Outlook Forum (GHACOF) technical statements** — most recent: GHACOF68 (2025) and GHACOF66 (2024); covers seasonal forecast methodology + ENSO state + regional consensus. [ICPAC GHACOF66 statement](https://www.icpac.net/publications/technical-statement-from-the-66th-greater-horn-of-africa-climate-outlook-forum-ghacof66/) · [GHACOF68](https://www.icpac.net/publications/technical-statement-from-the-68th-greater-horn-of-africa-climate-outlook-forum-ghacof68/)
  - **Seasonal forecasts** — March-May, June-September, October-December bulletins. Use of WMO objective forecasting procedure; documents which Global Producing Centres ICPAC consults (9 GPCs as of 2024).
  - **CONFER project** (Co-production of Climate Services for East Africa) — collaboration with European partners. [CONFER](https://www.icpac.net/our-projects/confer/)
- **Access**: [ICPAC publications](https://www.icpac.net/publications/)
- **What to cite this for**: The East Africa Paradox section especially — ICPAC's operational view; how the seasonal forecast process handles model uncertainty; that the region's NMHSs already use CMIP6.

### SADC-CSC — SADC Climate Services Centre

- **Location**: Gaborone, Botswana. Southern African Development Community.
- **Type**: WMO-designated Regional Climate Centre for Southern Africa
- **Most relevant publications**: Southern African Regional Climate Outlook Forum (SARCOF) technical statements; SADC State of the Climate reports *(outreach pending)*
- **Access**: [SADC SCC page](https://www.sadc.int/themes/disaster-risk-management/) (the climate services material is under SADC's Disaster Risk Management portfolio)
- **What to cite this for**: Southern African climate-services framing; CMIP-evaluation reports; partner perspective for the AFR-WSAF + AFR-ESAF regions.

### AGRHYMET Regional Centre

- **Location**: Niamey, Niger. CILSS (Permanent Inter-State Committee for Drought Control in the Sahel).
- **Type**: Specialised regional centre for agro-meteorology + hydrology, West Africa Sahel
- **Most relevant publications**: Sahel agricultural-season monitoring bulletins; CMIP-derived climate-impact studies for crop production *(outreach pending)*
- **Access**: [AGRHYMET](https://www.agrhymet.cilss.int/)
- **What to cite this for**: West Africa / Sahel perspective; agro-met framing relevant to the Atlas's agricultural audience; specific to monsoon-driven systems.

### CSAG — Climate System Analysis Group, University of Cape Town

- **Location**: Cape Town, South Africa.
- **Type**: Academic research group that operates as a regional centre of CMIP / CORDEX evaluation expertise for Southern Africa
- **Most relevant publications**: Bruce Hewitson + colleagues' extensive CMIP / CORDEX evaluation literature (cited in v0.1 references via Pinto, Lim Kam Sian, Engelbrecht et al.). Methodology development for downscaling specifically for Africa.
- **Access**: [CSAG](https://www.csag.uct.ac.za/)
- **What to cite this for**: Authoritative academic voice on dynamical downscaling over Southern Africa. The natural "Southern Africa" partner for the per-region evaluation discussion.

## CGIAR programmes (Atlas-adjacent)

### AICCRA — Accelerating Impacts of CGIAR Climate Research for Africa

- **Type**: CGIAR / World Bank-funded climate-information services programme for African agriculture
- **Most relevant publications**: AICCRA technical deliverables on climate-projection use in African agriculture; country-level adaptation analyses
- **Access**: [AICCRA](https://aiccra.cgiar.org/)
- **What to cite this for**: The closest CGIAR analogue to what the Atlas is doing. Useful for the "how this fits in the CGIAR landscape" framing and for AICCRA-published partner-country analyses.

### CGIAR Climate Action / Climate Data Hub itself

- **Type**: The platform hosting this wiki + adjacent climate-rationale tools
- **What to cite this for**: Self-reference. The wiki frames the Data Hub's overall offering.
- **Access**: [climate-action.cgiar.org](https://climate-action.cgiar.org/)

### CCAFS legacy publications (CGIAR Research Program on Climate Change, Agriculture and Food Security; 2011-2021)

- **Type**: Now-closed CGIAR research programme; produced a large body of African climate-information work
- **Most relevant publications**: CCAFS Climate Information Services Working Papers (multiple, ~2018-2023); regional analyses for East / West / Southern Africa
- **Access**: [CCAFS archive at CGSpace](https://cgspace.cgiar.org/)
- **What to cite this for**: Foundational CGIAR-side framing of climate-services use in African agriculture; methodology precedents.

## Global organisations (often Atlas-audience-facing)

### IPCC AR6 + Atlas

- **Type**: Authority of last resort. Reference often.
- **Most relevant publications**: AR6 WGI SPM, Chapters 1, 3, 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, Atlas; AR6 WGII Chapter 9 (Africa); Interactive Atlas
- **Access**: [IPCC AR6](https://www.ipcc.ch/assessment-report/ar6/) · [Interactive Atlas](https://interactive-atlas.ipcc.ch/)
- **What to cite this for**: Headline assessment framing; assessed ECS range (Ch 7); regional climate change information (Ch 10, 11, Atlas); African impacts (WGII Ch 9).

### WCRP — World Climate Research Programme

- **Type**: International body that coordinates CMIP
- **Most relevant publications**: CMIP6 design papers (Eyring 2016, ScenarioMIP for CMIP6); CMIP7 Fast Track design documents
- **Access**: [WCRP-CMIP](https://wcrp-cmip.org/)
- **What to cite this for**: That CMIP is a coordinated international effort; CMIP7 timeline; the scientific-community governance behind the data.

### World Bank Climate Knowledge Portal (CCKP)

- **Type**: World Bank-operated public portal for country-level climate data
- **Most relevant publications**: CCKP methodology documentation; country profiles for African nations
- **Access**: [CCKP](https://climateknowledgeportal.worldbank.org/)
- **What to cite this for**: The most-likely alternative tool the Atlas audience already knows; useful for explicit "comparison with CCKP" framing in the dataset-landscape page.

### Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S)

- **Type**: EU operational climate service
- **Most relevant publications**: ESOTC (European State of the Climate); methodology documentation for the bias-adjusted CMIP6 dataset hosted at the Copernicus CDS; regional climate projections explainer
- **Access**: [climate.copernicus.eu](https://climate.copernicus.eu/) · [Copernicus CDS](https://cds.climate.copernicus.eu/)
- **What to cite this for**: That alternative bias-adjusted CMIP6 datasets exist beyond NEX-GDDP-CMIP6; methodology comparison.

### FAO climate publications

- **Type**: UN Food and Agriculture Organization
- **Most relevant publications**: FAO's Climate Change & Food Security series; specific reports on African agro-climatic risk
- **Access**: [FAO climate change](https://www.fao.org/climate-change/en/)
- **What to cite this for**: Agricultural-climate framing relevant to the Atlas's agricultural audience.

### GCF — Green Climate Fund

- **Type**: International climate fund the Atlas's primary audience writes proposals to
- **Most relevant publications**: GCF Investment Criteria; Initial Investment Framework; technical-guidance documents for concept-note preparation
- **Access**: [GCF documents](https://www.greenclimate.fund/document)
- **What to cite this for**: What GCF reviewers expect to see; how climate science gets translated to proposal language; the operational reason the Atlas exists.

### Adaptation Fund

- **Type**: UNFCCC-aligned adaptation funder
- **Most relevant publications**: Project Design Document templates; technical guidance for proposal preparation
- **Access**: [Adaptation Fund](https://www.adaptation-fund.org/)
- **What to cite this for**: Parallel audience to GCF; alternative funding framework the same proposal authors use.

### UNFCCC NDC Registry

- **Type**: National Determined Contribution submissions from African countries
- **What's here**: How African nations themselves are using climate projections in their NDCs (which datasets they cite, which scenarios they use)
- **Access**: [UNFCCC NDC Registry](https://unfccc.int/NDCREG)
- **What to cite this for**: Concrete examples of how the Atlas's outputs are *currently being used* in policy submissions. Search the NDC of any African country and you'll find climate-projection references the Atlas could either complement or replace.

## Recurring gaps to flag in the wiki

When writing, where grey lit is missing, the wiki should be explicit:

- **No published continental-scale CMIP6 evaluation by ACMAD** as of 2026-05. If they've done one, they haven't surfaced it through standard discovery channels. The wiki should flag this gap and offer ACMAD a place to fill it.
- **SADC-CSC's published methodology is sparse online.** The CMIP-evaluation work is primarily in NMHS-internal documents. Partner outreach is the only path to surface this.
- **AGRHYMET specialises in agro-met operational products** rather than long-range CMIP-evaluation publications. Acknowledge this; cite their operational role rather than expecting publication output.
- **AICCRA deliverables are extensive but not always indexed via standard search.** CGSpace is the place to look for them.
