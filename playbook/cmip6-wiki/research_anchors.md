# Research anchors — CMIP6 Africa wiki v2

This is the citation backbone for the v2 multi-page wiki. For each foundational topic the wiki covers, this worksheet lists the 3-5 best peer-reviewed sources, the 2-3 best grey-lit anchors, and a one-sentence "what to cite this for" note. Writing each page draws from this list — no claim in the wiki should appear without a source in this worksheet (or a clear reason it's an original Atlas synthesis).

**Citation convention** — when writing a wiki page, link inline as `[Author et al. YEAR](DOI URL)` or `[Org name](URL)`. Don't number footnotes; flat hyperlinks are easier to maintain.

**Last updated**: 2026-05-28 (initial seeding from web research + the v0.1 wiki's reference list)

## A1. What is a future climate projection?

**Audience hook**: "I keep seeing climate-projection charts. What am I actually looking at?"

### Primary

- **IPCC AR6 WGI Summary for Policymakers** (2021). The headline assessment authority. Cite for "what counts as a projection" and confidence levels. [IPCC AR6 WGI SPM](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/downloads/report/IPCC_AR6_WGI_SPM.pdf)
- **IPCC AR6 WGI Chapter 1** — Framing, Context, and Methods. Useful for the conceptual definition of a projection vs prediction vs scenario. [AR6 WGI Ch 1](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-1/)
- **IPCC AR6 WGI Atlas** — regional climate change. The "what does it look like for Africa specifically" reference. Interactive Atlas data is the partner-friendly access point. [AR6 WGI Atlas Chapter](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/atlas/) · [Interactive Atlas](https://interactive-atlas.ipcc.ch/)
- **Hawkins & Sutton (2009)** "The potential to narrow uncertainty in regional climate predictions." *Bull. Am. Meteorol. Soc.* Three sources of uncertainty (model, scenario, internal variability) — foundational framing. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1175/2009BAMS2607.1)

### Grey lit / institutional

- **WMO State of the Climate in Africa 2024** (WMO-No. 1349). The most recent continental authority. [WMO SOC Africa 2024](https://wmo.int/publication-series/state-of-climate-africa-2024)
- **WMO State of the Climate in Africa 2023** (WMO-No. 1360). Useful for trend continuity. [PDF on UNECA-ACPC](https://www.uneca.org/sites/default/files/ACPC/publication/State-of-the-Climate-in-Africa-2023_en.pdf)
- **Copernicus C3S "What is climate change?" series** — accessible orientation pieces partners trust. [Copernicus climate explainers](https://climate.copernicus.eu/climate-resources)
- **AR6 Interactive Atlas methodology page** (Gutiérrez et al. 2021) — useful for the "how the IPCC presents this" framing. [AR6 Atlas explainer](https://interactive-atlas.ipcc.ch/)

### What to cite this section for

- The headline definition of a "future climate projection" (AR6 Ch 1).
- That projections are scenario-dependent (SSPs) and that scenario uncertainty is one of three uncertainty axes (Hawkins & Sutton).
- The current observed-climate context for Africa (WMO SOC Africa 2024).

## A2. What is a Global Climate Model (GCM)?

**Audience hook**: "What's inside these models I'm citing?"

### Primary

- **Eyring et al. (2016)** "Overview of the Coupled Model Intercomparison Project Phase 6 (CMIP6) experimental design and organisation." *Geosci. Model Dev.* The canonical paper introducing CMIP6. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-9-1937-2016)
- **Hourdin et al. (2017)** "The art and science of climate model tuning." *Bull. Am. Meteorol. Soc.* Why each model is slightly different — different choices made by each modelling centre. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1175/BAMS-D-15-00135.1)
- **IPCC AR6 WGI Chapter 1** — Section 1.5 on climate models, model hierarchies, and intercomparison projects. [AR6 WGI Ch 1](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-1/)
- **Flato et al. (2013)** "Evaluation of Climate Models." In *AR5 WGI Ch 9*. Although AR5-era, the chapter remains the best textbook-style introduction to what an Earth System Model is and how it's evaluated. [AR5 WGI Ch 9](https://www.ipcc.ch/site/assets/uploads/2018/02/WG1AR5_Chapter09_FINAL.pdf)
- **AR6 WGI Chapter 3** (Eyring et al. 2021) — Human Influence on the Climate System. Section 3.3 on model evaluation gives the AR6-era picture. [AR6 WGI Ch 3](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-3/)

### Grey lit / institutional

- **WCRP CMIP overview page** — describes the CMIP framework and its phases. [WCRP-CMIP](https://wcrp-cmip.org/)
- **Met Office "What is a climate model?"** explainer. Friendly for non-specialists. [Met Office explainer](https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/research/approach/modelling-systems/unified-model/climate-models)
- **NCAS-Climate primer materials** (UK National Centre for Atmospheric Science). [NCAS](https://ncas.ac.uk/)
- **Carbon Brief explainer: "How climate models work"** — widely cited friendly overview. [Carbon Brief](https://www.carbonbrief.org/qa-how-do-climate-models-work)

### What to cite this section for

- The CMIP6 framework and its component MIPs (Eyring 2016).
- Why models differ at the design level (Hourdin 2017 on tuning).
- That ~50+ models contributed to CMIP6, of which 18 are downscaled in NEX-GDDP (Atlas wiki connection).

## A3. Why do GCMs disagree?

**Audience hook**: "If models are based on physics, why don't they all give the same answer?"

### Primary

- **Hawkins & Sutton (2009)** — the three-source uncertainty framing (model / scenario / internal variability). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1175/2009BAMS2607.1)
- **Knutti & Sedláček (2013)** "Robustness and uncertainties in the new CMIP5 climate model projections." *Nat. Clim. Change*. The "why is the spread the size it is" reference. Still applies to CMIP6. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/nclimate1716)
- **Knutti et al. (2013)** "Climate model genealogy: Generation CMIP5 and how we got there." *Geophys. Res. Lett.* Models inherit code lineages; their disagreements aren't independent samples. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/grl.50256)
- **Sanderson et al. (2015)** "A representative democracy to reduce interdependency in a multimodel ensemble." *J. Climate*. The ensemble-weighting / model-democracy debate. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1175/JCLI-D-14-00362.1)
- **AR6 WGI Chapter 4** (Lee et al. 2021) — Future Global Climate: Scenario-Based Projections. Sections on inter-model spread and constrained projections. [AR6 WGI Ch 4](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-4/)
- **Sherwood et al. (2020)** "An assessment of Earth's climate sensitivity using multiple lines of evidence." *Rev. Geophys.* The basis for AR6's narrowed ECS range. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2019RG000678)

### Grey lit / institutional

- **Carbon Brief explainer on climate sensitivity and the hot model problem**. [Carbon Brief hot models](https://www.carbonbrief.org/explainer-how-scientists-estimate-climate-sensitivity)
- **AR6 Interactive Atlas methodology** — explains how AR6 handles inter-model uncertainty for regional information. [AR6 Atlas](https://interactive-atlas.ipcc.ch/)
- **Tebaldi & Knutti (2007)** "The use of the multi-model ensemble in probabilistic climate projections." *Phil. Trans. R. Soc. A*. Classic theoretical framing. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1098/rsta.2007.2076)

### What to cite this section for

- The three uncertainty axes (Hawkins & Sutton).
- That model spread is partially structural, not just sampling noise (Knutti 2013).
- That CMIP6's range is informed by — but not identical to — AR6's *assessed* range (Sherwood 2020; AR6 Ch 4 on constrained projections).

## A4. Downscaling — statistical vs dynamical

**Audience hook**: "What does NEX-GDDP actually do to the raw model output? And how is that different from CORDEX?"

### Primary

- **Maraun & Widmann (2018)** *Statistical Downscaling and Bias Correction for Climate Research*. Cambridge University Press. The textbook. [Cambridge UP](https://doi.org/10.1017/9781107588783)
- **Gutiérrez et al. (2019)** "An intercomparison of a large ensemble of statistical downscaling methods over Europe." *Int. J. Climatol.* Method-comparison framing. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1002/joc.5462)
- **Giorgi & Gutowski (2015)** "Regional dynamical downscaling and the CORDEX initiative." *Annu. Rev. Environ. Resour.* The canonical CORDEX overview. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-environ-102014-021217)
- **Thrasher et al. (2022)** "NASA Global Daily Downscaled Projections, CMIP6." *Sci. Data*. The NEX-GDDP-CMIP6 paper. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-022-01393-4)
- **Lange (2019)** "Trend-preserving bias adjustment and statistical downscaling with ISIMIP3BASD (v1.0)." *Geosci. Model Dev.* The ISIMIP3 method paper — also underpins CHELSA-CMIP6. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-12-3055-2019)
- **AR6 WGI Chapter 10** (Doblas-Reyes et al. 2021) — Linking Global to Regional Climate Change. The AR6 chapter on regionalisation methods. [AR6 WGI Ch 10](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-10/)

### Grey lit / institutional

- **CORDEX project documentation** — domains, methods, archiving specs. [CORDEX](https://cordex.org/)
- **NASA NEX-GDDP-CMIP6 product page** — technical doc + data access. [NASA NEX-GDDP-CMIP6](https://www.nccs.nasa.gov/services/data-collections/land-based-products/nex-gddp-cmip6)
- **CHELSA-CMIP6 product page** — methodology overview. [CHELSA-CMIP6](https://chelsa-climate.org/cmip6/)
- **ISIMIP framework documentation** — input data + method. [ISIMIP](https://www.isimip.org/)
- **AR6 WGI Atlas — regional information explainer**. [AR6 Atlas](https://interactive-atlas.ipcc.ch/)

### What to cite this section for

- Statistical-vs-dynamical contrast (Maraun & Widmann 2018; Giorgi & Gutowski 2015).
- NEX-GDDP-CMIP6's specific BCSD methodology (Thrasher 2022).
- ISIMIP3BASD as the ISIMIP3 / CHELSA-CMIP6 method (Lange 2019).
- That CORDEX is dynamically downscaled; NEX-GDDP / ISIMIP / CHELSA are statistical.

## A5. Bias correction

**Audience hook**: "What does 'bias corrected' actually mean, and should I trust it?"

### Primary

- **Cannon et al. (2015)** "Bias correction of GCM precipitation by quantile mapping: How well do methods preserve changes in quantiles and extremes?" *J. Climate*. The methods-comparison reference. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1175/JCLI-D-14-00754.1)
- **Ehret et al. (2012)** "Should we apply bias correction to global and regional climate model data?" *Hydrol. Earth Syst. Sci.* The "don't always do it" caveat paper. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-16-3391-2012)
- **Maraun (2016)** "Bias correcting climate change simulations — a critical review." *Curr. Clim. Change Rep.* The review of methods + their pitfalls. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40641-016-0050-x)
- **Lange (2019)** — ISIMIP3BASD method, as above. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-12-3055-2019)
- **Wood et al. (2004)** "Hydrologic implications of dynamical and statistical approaches to downscaling climate model outputs." *Clim. Change*. The original BCSD method paper. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1023/B:CLIM.0000013685.99609.9e)

### Grey lit / institutional

- **World Bank Climate Knowledge Portal — methodology documentation**. Explains what bias correction means at the portal level. [WB CKP methodology](https://climateknowledgeportal.worldbank.org/about)
- **Copernicus Climate Data Store — bias-adjusted CMIP6 dataset documentation**. [Copernicus CDS bias-adjusted CMIP6](https://cds.climate.copernicus.eu/datasets)
- **AR6 WGI Cross-Chapter Box 12.1** — bias adjustment and "lurking quantities." Cite for caveats. [AR6 WGI Ch 12](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-12/)

### What to cite this section for

- The motivation for bias correction (closing the gap between raw model output and observed climate; Cannon 2015).
- The risks (over-correction, breaking physical consistency; Ehret 2012; Maraun 2016).
- The specific methods used in different Atlas-relevant datasets (BCSD in NEX-GDDP; ISIMIP3BASD in ISIMIP3 / CHELSA).

## A6. The "hot model" problem and constrained projections

**Audience hook**: "Why is CanESM5 excluded from your default ensemble?"

### Primary

- **Hausfather, Marvel, Schmidt, Nielsen-Gammon, Zelinka (2022)** "Climate simulations: recognize the 'hot model' problem." *Nature* 605, 26-29. The canonical citation. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/d41586-022-01192-2)
- **Forster et al. (2021)** "The Earth's Energy Budget, Climate Feedbacks, and Climate Sensitivity." In *AR6 WGI Ch 7*. The AR6 ECS assessment. [AR6 WGI Ch 7](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-7/)
- **Sherwood et al. (2020)** — climate-sensitivity assessment via multiple lines of evidence. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1029/2019RG000678)
- **Brunner et al. (2020)** "Reduced global warming from CMIP6 projections when weighting models by performance and independence." *Earth Syst. Dynam.* [DOI](https://doi.org/10.5194/esd-11-995-2020)
- **Tokarska et al. (2020)** "Past warming trend constrains future warming in CMIP6 models." *Sci. Adv.* The observation-constrained projection paper. [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aaz9549)
- **Bloch-Johnson, Rugenstein, Gregory, Cael & Andrews (2022)** "Climate impact assessments should not discount 'hot' models." *Nature* 608, 667 (commentary, counter-view to Hausfather 2022). [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/d41586-022-02241-6)

### Grey lit / institutional

- **AR6 WGI SPM Box B.5** on assessed ECS and TCR ranges. [AR6 SPM](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/downloads/report/IPCC_AR6_WGI_SPM.pdf)
- **AR6 Interactive Atlas — constrained projections explainer**. [AR6 Atlas](https://interactive-atlas.ipcc.ch/)
- **Carbon Brief explainer on hot models**. [Carbon Brief hot models](https://www.carbonbrief.org/qa-the-controversy-over-climate-models-running-too-hot/)

### What to cite this section for

- Hausfather 2022 for the binary exclusion argument.
- Sherwood 2020 + AR6 Ch 7 for the assessed ECS range that puts CanESM5 outside the very-likely band.
- Tebaldi 2022 for the counter-argument that we honour by keeping FULL-18 available.

## Notes for the writer

1. **Diversity of voices** — the wiki should cite both primary research and grey-lit (regional centres, funders, IPCC). Don't over-rely on the same 5 papers across multiple sections.
2. **Acknowledge what's missing** — if a topic has *no* good African-specific grey lit, say so. Honesty calibration matters.
3. **Updateability** — when CMIP7 results land in 2026-2027, sections A8 and the model-evaluation pages will need refreshing. Build the wiki structure to make that a quick task.
4. **Links go stale** — every URL above should be re-checked at publish time. The CDH site auto-builds, so broken-link checking should be part of the publish workflow.
5. **Paywalls** — most papers above are open-access (especially the recent Copernicus / Frontiers / AMS ones). A few might paywall; cite the DOI either way.
