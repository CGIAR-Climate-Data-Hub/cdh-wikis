This section collects the Climate Data Hub's reference material on **how to choose climate-model ensembles** for African regional adaptation work — which models perform well over which sub-region, why we drop the "hot model" CanESM5, and how to handle the East African Paradox honestly in a climate rationale.

These pages originated in work on the [AAA Adaptation Atlas](https://adaptationatlas.cgiar.org/)'s *Build a Climate Rationale* notebook. That notebook surfaces future-climate projections at country and admin-1 level for sub-Saharan Africa and Madagascar to support proposal authors, NAP teams, and CGIAR programme designers. To do that defensibly, we needed a clear public answer to *which CMIP6 ensembles can we use for each region* — these wikis are that answer. Although the methodology was first applied in the Atlas, the choices apply to any African climate-rationale work using NEX-GDDP-CMIP6.

Audience: educated user who isn't necessarily a climate-science specialist — typically a climate-rationale author for a Green Climate Fund (GCF) or Adaptation Fund concept note, a national NAP submission, or a research-for-development scenario brief.
