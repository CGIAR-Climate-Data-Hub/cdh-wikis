The **Africa Agriculture Adaptation Atlas (AAA Atlas)** publishes country and admin-1 level climate-adaptation analytics for sub-Saharan Africa and Madagascar. These wikis explain the methodology choices behind what you see in the Atlas — *why* we use the data sources we use, *why* we make the model choices we make, and *what that means for your reading*.

Audience: educated user who isn't necessarily a climate-science specialist — typically a climate-rationale author for a Green Climate Fund (GCF) or Adaptation Fund concept note, a national NAP submission, or a research-for-development scenario brief.

## Current wikis

- **[African CMIP6 Ensembling](/wikis/aaa-atlas/african-cmip6-ensembling/)** — How the AAA Atlas selects climate-model ensembles for African regions; why per-region tuning matters; what the East African Paradox means for projection confidence; what's coming next from CMIP7 and CORDEX-Africa.

## How to use these wikis

When you cite the Atlas in a proposal or report, link the relevant wiki here as your methodology reference. The wikis are designed to be defensible answers to common reviewer questions:

- *"Why these climate models, not others?"* → [African CMIP6 Ensembling](/wikis/aaa-atlas/african-cmip6-ensembling/)
- *(More to come as the Atlas team commissions further reference material — open an issue if there's a topic you'd like covered.)*
